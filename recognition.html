    • Recognition

    Usuability Evaluation of the University of Leicester website through Neilsen's 10 Heuristics

    The primary problem with using the breadcrumbs for navigation (on this site) is that often links on a level two or three page will suddenly move you to a subpage in a completely different category. For example, a breadcrumb link on the titled “Academic Departments” (which is a level two category) may move you to the level three page “Academic Departments A-Z” in the “Indexes” category. Along with a slightly different column alignment and a completely different color scheme, the breadcrumb trail has changed to the “Indexes” category. This transformation renders them useless in returning you to the previous page you were visiting.

    The use of different background colors to delineate each of the different level two categories can be a helpful indicator as to where you are, but falls flat for one main reason. The different categories have far too many references to one another. It seems like almost every page contains links that take you to a completely different category and with it, a new background. This means that the background color is constantly changing as you navigate through the site, so it becomes less of an indicator and more of an inconsistent aesthetic choice.
