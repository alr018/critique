Aesthetics and Minimalist Design

Information should be displayed in a way that is clean and concise.

The website lacks consistency in many categories

The homepage feels very clean and minimalist (especially when compared to other university sites). It has a large image carousel with a few clear links on each of the carousel tabs. As you scroll down, the recent articles are displayed in a row that can be navigated through with right and left arrow buttons, which cue the user that there is additional information to display. Below that there are four common links each cleanly attached to a related image that take the user to a few of the most common sub-sites.

By stripping out a lot of the extra links and text, they have made the homepage rather aesthetically appealing, but it did come at a cost. The removal of links means that the homepage does not always clearly direct the user to where he can find the information that he is looking for. This likely forces them to use the search functionality, which is not ideal.

Once you progress into the level two and lower pages, the aesthetic feel of the homepage is lost. Most pages suffer from extreme overcrowding. There are often two horizontal navigation bars (one for general navigation and one for breadcrumbs), two side columns with links and one main column for content. Each one of these sections has a superfluous amount of text and hyper-links. This can make the page distracting for the user because his attention is pulled in many different directions, often away from the main content.
