Consistency

Usuability Evaluation of the University of Leicester website through Neilsen's 10 Heuristics

The website lacks consistency in many categories

For navigation, the home page looks nothing like any other page, it feels that we go through two different websites. If we research something, we go to the google page, embedded in the website, but it does not feel like the navigation is consistent.

Headlines are not consistent either. for example, “Our Research” leads to “Research”, “Business & Enterprise” leads to “Business Services”.

Even the buttons do not seem to be consistent: search button are much more oval than letter buttons

The contact bar at the bottom is not consistent. In the home page, it is relatively tall and black. On the “Our Research” page, it is much shorter, but still black.Finally, on the sports page, the contact bar is short and green.

The home page presents the website’s brand as colorful, simplistic, with not too much information. However, the rest of the website looks old fashioned, it has too much text everywhere, almost no images, blank backgrounds, etc.
